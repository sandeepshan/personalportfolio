Decision-first analytics

What I believe

Five principles I won’t compromise on

Not a framework. Not a methodology. Just the things I’ve learned the hard way and held onto.

01

Data without a decision is just storage

Every dashboard, model, and pipeline should trace back to a decision someone needs to make. If you can’t answer “what changes when someone sees this?” — stop building it. The world has enough beautiful charts nobody acts on.

02

The business problem always comes first

Technology is a means, not a destination. I’ve seen organisations spend years modernising platforms while the same broken questions go unanswered. Start with what the business is trying to decide. Everything else follows.

03

Trust is the only currency that matters

Executives don’t use data they don’t trust — and they’re right not to. Data governance isn’t bureaucracy; it’s the foundation every analysis, model, and conversation depends on. An organisation that trusts its data moves faster than one that doesn’t. Full stop.

04

Teams beat platforms, every time

No technology has ever built a data culture. People do. The best investment is the one that makes your team trusted by the business — not the one that impresses a vendor. A motivated, aligned team with average tools will outperform a brilliant platform with disengaged people, always.

05

AI amplifies judgment — it doesn’t replace it

The leaders who will thrive in an AI-augmented world aren’t the ones who know the most about AI — they’re the ones who ask the best questions. Curiosity and critical thinking compound. Prompt-following doesn’t.
