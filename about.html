About

From building reports to
building the teams that build them

My career began as a Senior BI Analyst, hands-on with the data itself — extracting, cleansing, and reporting it for businesses that didn't yet trust it to make decisions on its own. Sixteen years later, I lead the function that does that work for an entire organisation, at a scale I couldn't have imagined from that first seat.

What's stayed constant is the belief that data only creates value the day someone outside the data team can act on it without asking for help. Governance, modern platforms, AI enablement — none of it matters if it doesn't end in someone, somewhere, making a better decision faster. That's the discipline I've built every team around, from a 25-person squad at Australia Post to the centralised Data, Analytics & AI function I run at iSelect today.

I've come to believe leadership in data is less about being the smartest person on the platform and more about being the person the business trusts to translate ambiguity into a plan. My teams have sustained employee engagement above 85% — not because the work is easy, but because people know what they're building toward and why it matters.
